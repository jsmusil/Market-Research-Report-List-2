<p><p>Food Grade Pectinase is a common enzyme used in the food industry for various purposes such as enhancing juice extraction, improving fruit processing and increasing clarity in wines. There are several types of pectinase enzymes including Protopectinase, Polygalacturonase, Pectin Lyase, and Pectinesterase. Protopectinase breaks down protopectin in fruits, Polygalacturonase breaks down pectin in cell walls, Pectin Lyase cleaves pectin molecules, and Pectinesterase removes methyl groups from pectin. Each type of pectinase plays a unique role in food processing and contributes to the overall quality of the final product.</p></p>
