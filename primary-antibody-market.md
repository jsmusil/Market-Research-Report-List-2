<p><p>Primary antibodies are essential tools in various research fields, with two main types being Monoclonal Antibodies and Polyclonal Antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are produced from identical immune cells and target a specific antigen, allowing for precise and consistent results. Polyclonal antibodies, on the other hand, are derived from multiple immune cells and recognize multiple epitopes on an antigen. Both types have their strengths and limitations, making them valuable assets in scientific research and diagnostics.</p></p>
