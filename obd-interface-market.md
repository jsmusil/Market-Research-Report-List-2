<p><strong>The OBD Interface Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>OBD-I</li><li>OBD-II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>OBD-I and OBD-II are two types of On-Board Diagnostics interfaces used in vehicles to monitor the performance and diagnose issues. OBD-I was introduced in the 1980s and had limited capabilities compared to OBD-II, which was mandated for all vehicles in the US from 1996 onwards. OBD-II offers more advanced features and standardization, making it easier for mechanics to troubleshoot problems. Both types of OBD interfaces have played a crucial role in improving vehicle diagnostics and maintenance processes.</p></p>
