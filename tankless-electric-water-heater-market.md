<p><ul><li>Whole-House</li><li>Point-of-Use</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Tankless electric water heaters come in two main market types: whole-house and point-of-use. Whole-house tankless water heaters are designed to provide hot water to an entire home and are typically installed centrally in a house. Point-of-use tankless water heaters, on the other hand, are smaller units that are installed near the specific point of hot water use, such as under a sink or in a bathroom. The choice between these two market types depends on the size of the household and specific hot water needs.</p></p>
