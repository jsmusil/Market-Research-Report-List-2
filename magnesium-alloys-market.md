<p><p>Magnesium alloys are divided into two main types - cast alloys and wrought alloys. Cast alloys are formed by pouring molten magnesium into molds, allowing for complex shapes and sizes to be easily produced. Wrought alloys, on the other hand, are formed through mechanical processes such as rolling, forging, or extrusion, resulting in improved mechanical properties. Both types of magnesium alloys have unique advantages and applications in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and electronics.</p></p>
