<p><p>Cerebral vascular stents are small, tube-like devices used to support blood vessels in the brain. They come in three main types: tantalum, stainless steel, and nitinol. Tantalum stents are highly biocompatible and resistant to corrosion. Stainless steel stents are durable and offer good flexibility. Nitinol stents are known for their shape memory and superelasticity, allowing for better adaptation to the blood vessels. Each type of stent has unique characteristics that make them suitable for different patient needs in the treatment of cerebral vascular conditions.</p></p>
