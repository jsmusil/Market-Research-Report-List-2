<p><p>Flax yarns can be categorized into two main market types based on the spinning process: wet spinning and dry spinning. In wet spinning, the flax fibers are processed with water during the spinning, resulting in a softer and more lustrous yarn. On the other hand, dry spinning involves spinning the flax fibers without the use of water, leading to a stiffer and more durable yarn. Both wet spinning and dry spinning cater to different consumer preferences and applications in the textile industry.</p></p>
