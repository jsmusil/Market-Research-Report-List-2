<p><p>Wire extension cords come in two main types: high voltage and low voltage. High voltage extension cords are designed for heavy-duty applications, such as industrial settings or large machinery, where a higher amount of power is needed. These cords typically have thicker wires and stronger insulation to handle the increased electrical load. Low voltage extension cords, on the other hand, are used for more common household applications, such as lamps or small appliances. They have thinner wires and are generally more lightweight and flexible for easier use in residential settings.</p></p>
