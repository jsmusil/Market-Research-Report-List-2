<p><p>Waste incinerators are classified into different types based on their design and operation. The four main types of waste incinerators are rotary kiln, fluidized bed, static hearth, and others. Rotary kiln incinerators use a rotating cylinder to efficiently burn waste. Fluidized bed incinerators use a bed of solid particles to burn waste at lower temperatures. Static hearth incinerators rely on a stationary hearth to burn waste. Other types of incinerators include multiple hearth, controlled air, and modular systems, each with unique features and benefits.</p></p>
