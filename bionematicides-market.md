<p><ul><li>Microbials</li><li>Biochemical</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bionematicides can be categorized into two main types: microbials and biochemicals. Microbials are biological agents that control nematode populations through various mechanisms such as parasitism, antibiosis, and competition. Biochemicals, on the other hand, are naturally occurring compounds that disrupt nematode behavior or metabolism. Both types of bionematicides offer environmentally friendly alternatives to conventional chemical nematicides, providing sustainable solutions for managing nematode infestations in agriculture. The bionematicides market is expected to see continued growth as more farmers embrace these eco-friendly alternatives.</p></p>
